Frequently Asked Questions

1. How do I determine the right dumpster size for my project?

Choosing the right size depends on your debris type and volume. A 10 cubic yard dumpster rental is great for heavy inert materials or small cleanouts, while a 30 yard roll off dumpster rental is better for whole-home cleanouts. We help customers nationwide assess their needs over the phone.

2. What items are prohibited in the dumpster?

For safety and environmental reasons, you cannot dispose of hazardous materials in our units. This includes batteries, paint, tires, asbestos, and flammable liquids in any garbage dumpster rental nationwide. We can provide a full list of prohibited items for your specific state.

3. How long can I keep the rental?

Our standard rental period is typically 7 to 14 days, depending on the region. However, we offer temporary dumpster rental extensions if your project takes longer. Just let us know your timeline when booking your roll off container rental nationwide.

4. Is there a weight limit for the dumpsters?

Yes, every dumpster box has a weight limit based on its size and local road laws. For example, a construction debris dumpster used for concrete will have different restrictions than one used for household trash. We will clarify the tonnage allowance for your rental nationwide.

5. Do I need to be present for delivery?

You generally do not need to be home for the drop-off of your roll off rental nationwide, provided the delivery area is clear and accessible. Please ensure no cars or gates are blocking the spot where you want the waste management bin rental placed.

6. Can I place the dumpster on the street?

Placing a roll off on a public street usually requires a permit from your local city or municipality. If you keep the unit on your private driveway, a permit is rarely needed. We advise checking local regulations for city dumpsters in your specific area.

Chatham, IL Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

Chatham is a village in Sangamon County, Illinois, United States. It is located 2.8 miles (4.5 km) south of Springfield and has a retail trade area that extends into four other municipalities, including Springfield. The population was 11,500 as of the 2010 census and estimated to be 13,008 as of 2019. The village lies along the original alignment of historic U.S. Route 66 (Illinois Route 4).
